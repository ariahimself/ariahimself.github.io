Hello I am Aria.<br>
I completed concurrent Ph.D.s in Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) and Mathematics at Northeastern University.<br>
In my ECE thesis (<a href="https://www.proquest.com/openview/11cbad9c930af1ef273dc2e064995337/1?pq-origsite=gscholar&cbl=18750&diss=y" style="color: inherit; text-decoration: none;">Making Deep Neural Networks Transparent</a>) I developed theoretical and practical methods to enhance the interpretability and reliability of machine learning models. My second Ph.D. thesis in Mathematics (<a href="https://www.proquest.com/openview/241e849a42b19c1fc4e25703823418d3/1?pq-origsite=gscholar&cbl=18750&diss=y" style="color: inherit; text-decoration: none;">Poisson Geometry of Flag Varieties and Representation Theory of Their Quantum Deformations</a>) explored questions in representation theory and received the Best Ph.D. Thesis Award.<br>
This unique path has given me a strong foundation at the intersection of rigorous theory and applied research.<br>
My research interests lie in trustworthy AI and high-dimensional statistics, where I aim to develop methods that make AI systems both more practical and theoretically sound for long-term, real-world deployment. Along the way, I’ve had the opportunity to pursue these questions through both rigorous theoretical work and hands-on experimentation and to share the results at venues like *NeurIPS*, *ICLR*, and *AISTATS*.
